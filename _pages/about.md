## About Me

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Georgia, where I conduct research in Human-Centered AI, AI safety, and intelligent systems. My work focuses on building trustworthy and responsible AI technologies, with particular interests in agentic AI security, machine learning, social computing, and human-centered system design.

Before joining UGA, I completed my M.S. in Computer Science at the University of Texas at Arlington, where I worked as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Database Exploration Lab (DBXLab) under Dr. Gautam Das. My research there focused on feature selection, approximate functional dependencies, and data mining techniques for high-dimensional datasets. Prior to graduate studies in the United States, I served as a Lecturer in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Chittagong University of Engineering and Technology (CUET), teaching courses including Operating Systems, Software Engineering, Computer Architecture, and C Programming.

My recent research explores the security and societal implications of AI systems. I am currently working on red-teaming agentic financial systems through prompt injection attacks on Google’s Agent Payments Protocol (AP2), investigating vulnerabilities in autonomous AI-driven transactions and proposing safer system designs. I have also contributed to projects involving AI fairness auditing, HCI research, social computing, and human-centered AI systems for creative communities.

More broadly, I am passionate about designing AI systems that are secure, transparent, and socially responsible while bridging technical innovation with human needs and ethical considerations.
